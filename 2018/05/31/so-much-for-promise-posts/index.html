still kicking

After college I felt like I earned my degree, but I was lacking in a few areas that weren’t conducive to professional pursuit of what I had studied, mainly motivation.
So, I ended up doing slightly tech related jobs, mostly support, eventually labor (which I loved (agriculture, construction, etc.)), but never put myself in a position where expectations were high for either party. This has changed.

It’s been nearly a year since I got back into studying/implementing/creating IT and six months to the day since I started applying for tech jobs.
I’m still excited about this stuff. I have to admit: I was worried “Take 2”s momentum would abate and end w/ me going back to trade work and labor. But I haven’t.

I’m happiest when learning. This present and future, if I’m responsible, require me to keep learning. I’m excited.

in the works

Okay! I did get my Raspberry Pi 3. I set it up w/ Raspbian and installed the Alexa stuff.
It totally works! However, unless the device registered w/ the project is listed as commercial, you lose access to the music player API.
Bummer, but it can probably be worked around, so that’s on deck, but first in line…

I set up a bird feeder. By next Wednesday (computer club meeting day) I should have the Raspberry Pi working as an IP camera for the feeder. The short goal is getting that working.
The longer goal is getting it to send me notifications when something is at the feeder that will include a picture of what triggered the motion sensor.
This’ll be done w/ a motion sensor, the camera, and who knows what else. I’m thinking AWS SNS, possibly lambda… the write up comes later. Right now it’s just fun to have the idea going. It’ll end up as a link on this blog if I can figure that out
too, mb just the pictures it takes.

For studies though I’m working with VMWare and Docker/K8s. I’m creating a devOps pipeline with a client as well using a dockerfile, so we can start a small test CI/CD for his business.

social networks

The hacking/computer club I joined has been such a boon. Talking w/ other folks about what we’re all learning and how we can use it to solve diverse challenges keeps the fire going. I’ve also started attending another maker/security
group in the area that’s mostly composed of IT professionals who want to talk about tech, but not necessarily work. It’s also been helpful in regard to imposter syndrome. Talking the talk and walking the walk with students and professionals
alike gives me practice sessions to discuss what I’m doing and learning. I feel like I belong and that goes a long way to keeping the interest high. It helps an immeasurable amount in interviews as well.

I’m still working on a few CTF threads. One called Bandit that’s improving my sysAd skills and more general challenges that the college club set up. Getting to flex math or web muscles has been fun and refreshing.
I’ve helped others solve some challenges I had struggled with at first too. Keeping current on news bulletin worthy hacks and tech giant gaffs has kept me focused.

So long for now.
